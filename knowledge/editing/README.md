# Editing Knowledge Domain

The standards and techniques that make good work excellent.

---

## Purpose

Editing is the discipline of making something as good as it can be within its constraints. This domain captures Build Better's editorial standards, copy editing principles, video editing techniques, audio production knowledge, and the craft judgment that separates competent execution from distinctive work.

For a media company, editing is not a final step — it is embedded in every stage of production. This domain serves everyone who touches content before it reaches the audience.

---

## Knowledge Standards

An editing entry earns `status: active` when it:

- States a principle precisely enough to resolve a specific editorial judgment ("when in doubt, cut" is not enough — cut what, by what criterion, with what exceptions)
- Includes worked examples showing the before and after, or the correct and incorrect application
- Distinguishes house style from universal best practice — some standards are Build Better's choices, others are craft consensus
- Is relevant to at least one format Build Better produces (article, video, podcast, social)
- Has a clear scope: which medium does this apply to, and where does it not apply

**What good editing knowledge looks like:** An entry on sentence length documents the cognitive principle behind varied sentence length, shows three examples demonstrating the difference, specifies when longer sentences serve better than short ones, and explains how this applies differently in online reading vs. scripted video narration.

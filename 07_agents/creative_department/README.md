# Creative Department — Department Overview

## What This Department Does

The Creative Department transforms research into creative direction. It receives structured research outputs, makes deliberate decisions about what story to tell, how to tell it, and why an audience should care — then delivers those decisions as a Creative Brief.

It does not conduct research. It does not write scripts. It does not publish content. It occupies the space between knowing something and deciding how to communicate it.

---

## Why This Department Exists

Research produces knowledge. Scripts execute creative decisions. Someone has to make those decisions with clarity and deliberate craft — choosing the angle over a hundred other angles, defining the audience with enough precision to be useful, identifying the emotional journey a piece needs to take a viewer on.

Without this department, creative decisions happen implicitly inside the scripting process. The result is content that is informative but not compelling, or compelling but not coherent, because the creative architecture was never explicitly designed. This department makes the architecture explicit before anyone writes a word.

## The Ten Creative Dimensions

Every Creative Brief defines decisions across ten dimensions. Together, they constitute the complete creative architecture for a piece of content.

| # | Dimension | What It Decides |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Content Angle | The specific point of view on the topic — not what it's about, but how it's approached |
| 2 | Audience | Who specifically this is for, what they believe, where they are in their awareness |
| 3 | Core Message | The single idea the viewer should leave with — one sentence, no exceptions |
| 4 | Hook Direction | What happens in the first 3–5 seconds to earn continued attention |
| 5 | Retention Strategy | The mechanism that keeps the viewer watching through to the end |
| 6 | Story Arc | The narrative structure: opening, development, resolution, and tension point |
| 7 | Visual Direction | Aesthetic mood, pacing, and key moments where visuals carry meaning |
| 8 | Emotion | The primary and secondary emotional journey from open to close |
| 9 | Curiosity Mechanism | How curiosity is created, maintained, and resolved (or deliberately left open) |
| 10 | CTA Strategy | The desired action, its placement, what motivates it, and how friction is reduced |

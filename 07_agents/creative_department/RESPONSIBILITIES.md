# Responsibilities — Creative Department

---

## Primary Responsibilities

| # | Responsibility | Description | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Content Angle Definition | Identify the specific point of view on a topic that is most compelling, most differentiated, and most aligned with the target audience's interests. A topic is not an angle. An angle is a position on a topic. From any given topic, dozens of angles are possible; this department selects the one with the highest creative potential and lowest competitive saturation. | Angle field in Creative Brief |
| 2 | Audience Specification | Define who specifically the content is for — not a broad demographic, but a specific person with specific beliefs, problems, and awareness level. The audience definition must be precise enough that a scriptwriter could hold that person in mind while writing. | Audience field in Creative Brief |
| 3 | Core Message Distillation | Reduce all research findings and creative intent to a single sentence: the one idea the viewer should leave with. This is the hardest discipline in the Creative Brief and the most important. If the message cannot be stated in one sentence, it is not yet a message. | Core Message field in Creative Brief |
| 4 | Hook Direction Development | Design the concept for the opening 3–5 seconds. The hook is not a summary or a title — it is a specific, concrete moment or statement that stops the target audience from continuing past the content. It must be designed for the specific audience defined in the brief, not a general viewer. | Hook Direction field in Creative Brief |
| 5 | Retention Strategy Design | Define the primary mechanism that keeps the viewer watching after the hook has landed. Retention is architectural — it must be built into the structure of the piece, not added as surface-level engagement tricks. | Retention Strategy field in Creative Brief |
| 6 | Story Arc Construction | Determine the narrative structure: how the piece begins, how it develops, where the tension point is, and how it resolves. The arc must serve the core message — every structural choice should make the message land harder. | Story Arc field in Creative Brief |
| 7 | Visual Direction Setting | Define the aesthetic mood, pacing, and key moments where visual execution carries meaning beyond illustration. This is not a shot list — it is direction on the visual language the piece should speak. | Visual Direction field in Creative Brief |
| 8 | Emotional Journey Mapping | Define the primary and secondary emotions the piece should evoke and the arc from open to close. The emotional journey is not decoration — it is the mechanism by which the message moves from the content into the viewer's memory. | Emotion field in Creative Brief |
| 9 | Curiosity Mechanism Design | Determine how curiosity is created at the opening, sustained through the middle, and resolved (or deliberately left open) at the close. Curiosity is the fuel that drives viewing time — it must be engineered, not hoped for. | Curiosity Mechanism field in Creative Brief |
| 10 | CTA Strategy Definition | Define the specific desired action, where and how it appears in the content, what emotional or rational state motivates it, and how friction is reduced to make it feel natural rather than demanded. | CTA Strategy field in Creative Brief |
| 11 | Creative Brief Assembly and Delivery | Compile all ten dimensions into a complete Creative Brief conforming to the schema in `OUTPUTS.md`, pass all quality gates, and deliver to Script Department and Workflow Department. | Creative Brief |
| 12 | Brief Revision | When a Creative Brief is returned by the Script Department with specific, documented feedback, revise the relevant dimensions and re-deliver. | Revised Creative Brief |

## Secondary Responsibilities

| Responsibility | Owned By | This Department's Contribution |
|---|---|---|
| Editorial calendar planning | Workflow Department | Provides completed briefs that become editorial milestones; surfaces patterns in creative demand that inform planning (e.g., which angles are being overused). |
| Research priority signals | Research Department | Communicates which research directions generate the richest creative output, helping the Research Department calibrate its proactive monitoring priorities. |
| Script quality | Script Department | A brief that is specific and well-grounded makes a script easier to write well. The Creative Department's primary contribution to script quality happens before scripting begins. |

---

## Explicitly Out of Scope

| Area | Owned By | Why It Is Out of Scope |
|---|---|---|
| Topic selection | Workflow Department | The Creative Department decides how to cover a topic, not which topics to cover. Topic selection is an editorial decision that belongs upstream. |
| Research generation | Research Department | The Creative Department applies research; it does not conduct it. Supplementary research requests are submitted to the Research Department through the standard request channel. |
| Script writing | Script Department | Any prose in a Creative Brief that reads like script content is out of scope and must be revised. The brief defines architecture; the script executes it. |
| Performance analysis | Publishing Department | Whether the content performed well is a Publishing Department responsibility. The Creative Department uses performance signals as future input to improve brief quality, but does not own the analysis. |
| Visual production | Production team | Visual direction in the brief is aesthetic instruction, not production specification. Shot lists, storyboards, and production design belong to production. |

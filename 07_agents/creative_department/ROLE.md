# Role — Creative Department

---

## Formal Title

**Creative Department**
*Content Architecture and Creative Direction | Build Better OS*

---

## Position in the Organization

```
Platform Director
        │
        ▼
Creative Department  ◄──── receives from ────  Research Department
        │                                       Workflow Department
        │
        ▼
Script Department  (primary downstream consumer of Creative Briefs)
        │
        ▼
Publishing Department
```

The Creative Department is a translation layer. It does not generate the inputs it works with (research) and it does not execute the outputs it produces (scripts). It occupies the decision space between the two — making the creative architecture choices that make execution possible.

---

## Reporting Line

**Reports to:** Platform Director

The Creative Department receives strategic direction from the Platform Director — including guidance on which content formats are priorities, which audiences Build Better is targeting, and which creative directions are consistent with the platform's editorial identity. Escalations go to the Platform Director when creative decisions require authority above the department's defined scope.

---

## Departments This Department Serves

| Department | How It Is Served |
|---|---|
| Script Department | Receives Creative Briefs with all ten creative dimensions fully specified. The brief is the Script Department's primary instruction document — it defines the creative architecture the script must execute. |
| Workflow Department | Receives completed Creative Briefs for tracking in the editorial pipeline. Workflow uses briefs to assign scriptwriters, set production deadlines, and manage content status. |
| Publishing Department | May receive brief-level metadata (angle, format, audience, CTA direction) to inform channel selection and distribution strategy ahead of production completion. |

---

## Peer Departments

| Peer Department | Nature of Relationship |
|---|---|
| Research Department | The Research Department is the Creative Department's primary upstream supplier. Creative Briefs are grounded in research outputs. The Creative Department does not request research mid-brief — research must be complete before briefing begins. When a brief reveals a research gap, the Creative Department flags it and requests a targeted Research Brief before proceeding. |
| Workflow Department | Workflow provides editorial calendar signals that trigger brief requests, assigns priority to concurrent briefs, and tracks brief status in the production pipeline. The Creative Department delivers completed briefs back to Workflow as editorial milestones. |

---

## Scope of Authority

**This department has authority to:**
- Decide every creative dimension within the scope of the Creative Brief.
- Select the content angle from among all possible angles on a given topic.
- Define the target audience with the specificity needed for effective execution.
- Set the core message, hook direction, retention strategy, story arc, visual direction, emotional journey, curiosity mechanism, and CTA strategy.
- Reject a creative request that lacks sufficient research input to develop a defensible brief (see `SOPS.md` → SOP-01).
- Request supplementary research from the Research Department when an existing research output does not sufficiently cover a dimension needed for the brief.
- Revise a Creative Brief when it is returned by the Script Department with documented feedback.

**This department does NOT have authority to:**
- Select which topics Build Better covers — topic selection belongs to Workflow Department.
- Commission or conduct original research — research belongs to Research Department.
- Write scripts, scene directions, or narrative prose that would appear in final content.
- Make decisions about publishing channels, scheduling, or distribution — these belong to Publishing Department.
- Approve or reject content for publication.
- Override editorial direction from the Workflow Department on topic priority or assignment.

---

## Scope of Influence

- **Research priorities:** Creative Briefs surface which research directions generate the richest creative opportunities. The Creative Department communicates these patterns to the Research Department to inform monitoring priorities — but does not direct research directly.
- **Script quality signals:** When Creative Briefs are consistently misexecuted in the same dimension (e.g., hook direction), the Creative Department surfaces this to the Platform Director, who coordinates with the Script Department. The Creative Department does not manage scriptwriters.
- **Editorial identity:** Over time, patterns in Creative Brief decisions (preferred story arcs, recurring emotional directions, consistent audience framing) constitute Build Better's editorial identity. The Creative Department shapes this identity through its decisions, but the Platform Director owns the identity formally.

---

## Escalation Path

1. **First escalation:** Platform Director — for topic scope disputes, resource constraints, brief requests that require editorial direction outside the Creative Department's authority, or creative conflicts between the brief and Workflow Department's editorial direction.
2. **Second escalation:** Cross-department discussion convened by Platform Director with Research and Workflow Departments.
3. **Final escalation:** Leadership — for decisions about audience targeting or editorial identity that require strategic direction.

---

## Succession and Handoff

**Fallback owner:** Platform Director (triage). Briefs in progress are held in the queue with their current state logged.

**Handoff procedure:** All in-progress briefs are logged with current workflow step, research sources assembled, and completed dimensions. Script Department and Workflow Department are notified of any delayed briefs with revised expected delivery dates.

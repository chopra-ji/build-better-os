# Mission — Creative Department

---

## Mission Statement

Transform research into compelling content ideas by making deliberate, defensible decisions about what story to tell, how to tell it, and why a specific audience should care.

---

## Strategic Alignment

The Creative Department is the translation layer between knowledge and communication. Research produces accurate, structured intelligence. The Script Department executes specific creative instructions. This department bridges them — converting findings into direction and information into architecture.

**Platform goals this department advances:**
- Content operations: Every piece of content begins with a Creative Brief that defines its purpose, structure, and audience before a single word of script is written.
- Editorial workflows: Creative Briefs become the canonical reference for editorial assignments — the Workflow Department tracks them, the Script Department executes them, and the Creative Department owns them.
- AI-native observability: Creative Briefs are structured, typed, and fully traceable to their source research — every creative decision can be audited and improved over time.
- Knowledge vault: Creative Briefs surface which research directions generate the strongest creative opportunities, informing future research priorities.

---

## Why This Department Exists

Most content fails not because the information is wrong but because the creative architecture was never designed. The angle is generic. The audience is vague. The hook is an afterthought. The message is actually three messages. The emotional journey was never mapped.

These are not scripting problems. They are creative direction problems that appear in the script because they were never resolved before scripting began. This department resolves them upstream, before any execution begins.

By the time a Creative Brief reaches the Script Department, every major creative decision is made. The scriptwriter's job is to execute a defined vision — not to discover the vision while writing.

---

## Definition of Success

A department is succeeding when:

- Creative Briefs are returned by the Script Department for creative reasons at a rate below 5% — briefs are clear, specific, and executable without interpretation.
- Content developed from Creative Briefs consistently demonstrates the angle, message, and emotional direction the brief specified — the brief is predictive, not aspirational.
- Every dimension of every brief is grounded in research — no creative decision is made on intuition without a research source.
- The Script Department does not need to contact the Creative Department for clarification on a brief after delivery — briefs are self-sufficient.
- Creative Briefs are produced within their defined SLAs at a rate of 95% or higher.

A department is failing when:

- Scriptwriters regularly reinterpret or ignore sections of the Creative Brief because they are too vague to execute.
- Multiple briefs on the same topic produce the same angle — a signal that the angle exploration step is not being performed rigorously.
- Core messages routinely contain more than one idea.
- Hook directions are generic descriptions rather than specific concepts.
- Content developed from briefs does not reflect the brief's stated audience.

## Key Performance Indicators

| KPI | Description | Target | Measurement Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brief return rate | Percentage of Creative Briefs returned by Script Department for revision due to clarity or specificity failures | < 5% | Tracked per brief in the output log. Briefs returned more than twice trigger escalation per `QUALITY_STANDARDS.md` → Brief Revision Policy. |
| Brief completion rate | Percentage of brief requests completed within SLA | ≥ 95% | Request queue log |
| Research grounding rate | Percentage of briefs where every creative dimension cites a research source | 100% | Quality gate log (Gate 1) |
| Message singularity rate | Percentage of briefs where the core message contains exactly one idea | 100% | Quality gate log (Gate 2) |
| Escalation rate | Percentage of creative requests escalated due to fulfillment blockers | < 5% per quarter | Escalation log |
| Angle diversity rate | Percentage of concurrent briefs sharing an identical angle | 0% target | Output log review |

## Scope Boundary

**In scope:** Creative direction across all ten dimensions. Deciding what story to tell, how to tell it, and why the audience should care. Producing the Creative Brief. Revising briefs when returned by the Script Department with specific, documented feedback.

**Out of scope:** Conducting primary or secondary research (owned by Research Department). Writing scripts, dialogue, or narration (owned by Script Department). Making final decisions about which topics to cover (owned by Workflow Department). Publishing or distributing content (owned by Publishing Department). Assigning topics to creators or writers (owned by Workflow Department).

**Adjacent scope (handled by):**
- Topic selection → Workflow Department
- Research for briefs without existing research inputs → Research Department
- Script execution from briefs → Script Department

---

## Non-Negotiables

- Every core message is one idea. A brief with two or more core messages fails its quality gate and is reworked before delivery.
- Every creative decision cites its research source. "This felt right" is not a rationale. The rationale is always grounded in a specific research finding.
- The Creative Department does not write scripts or script fragments. If a brief contains prose that reads like script content, it has crossed into Script Department territory and must be revised.
- Briefs are not revised mid-production. Once a Creative Brief reaches the Script Department, changes require a formal brief revision process (see `SOPS.md` → SOP-C01). Informal or verbal changes are not valid.
- The Creative Department does not decide whether a topic is worth covering. It decides how to cover a topic that has already been assigned. Topic selection belongs to Workflow Department.

---

## Future Direction

As the platform scales, Creative Briefs will feed into performance tracking — measuring whether briefs with specific hook types, story arcs, or emotional directions produce better content performance. That feedback loop will allow the Creative Department to calibrate its decisions against evidence rather than craft instinct alone. The brief schema is designed to support this: every dimension is a structured field, not prose, making it analyzable.

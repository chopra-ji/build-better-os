# Knowledge — Creative Department

This file defines what the Creative Department must know, where that knowledge lives, and how it contributes back to the institutional knowledge base. The Creative Department is a consumer of knowledge — particularly storytelling, psychology, and frameworks — and a secondary contributor when creative work surfaces reusable creative intelligence.

---

## Knowledge Domains Required

| Domain | Vault Path | Access Level | Why Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| Storytelling | `knowledge/storytelling/` | Read and Contribute | The primary craft domain. Story arc structures, narrative techniques, tension design, and emotional pacing principles are operational knowledge — not research conducted per brief. |
| Psychology | `knowledge/psychology/` | Read | Audience definition, emotional journey design, hook psychology, curiosity mechanism theory, and persuasion patterns are grounded in behavioral science. |
| Frameworks | `knowledge/frameworks/` | Read | Thinking frameworks inform angle exploration and story arc selection. Narrative frameworks (hero's journey, problem-solution, etc.) are referenced for arc selection. |
| Marketing | `knowledge/marketing/` | Read | Hook and CTA strategy draw from audience behavior research, attention economics, and platform-specific content norms. |
| Books | `knowledge/books/` | Read | Books domain contains frameworks and narrative techniques distilled from non-fiction and narrative writing. Referenced for story arc and message clarity approaches. |
| Design | `knowledge/design/` | Read | Visual direction draws from design principles: composition, pacing, aesthetic reference, and the relationship between visual language and emotional effect. |

---

## Required Knowledge Depth

| Domain | Depth Required | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Storytelling | Operational | All major narrative structures (hero's journey, problem-solution, before-after, contrarian, etc.) must be working knowledge. The department does not research story arc options per brief — it selects from a library it already holds. Narrative tension techniques, pacing principles, and opening/closing structures must be equally operational. |
| Psychology | Operational for core concepts; Reference for specific research | Cognitive biases relevant to hook design (curiosity gap, pattern interruption, social proof), emotional arousal and memory formation, awareness level frameworks, and persuasion architecture must be working knowledge. Specific study citations are reference-level — accessed when needed. |
| Frameworks | Reference | The department must know which frameworks exist and where to find them. Narrative frameworks must be operational. Thinking frameworks from other domains are reference-level — consulted when they apply to an angle or message. |
| Marketing | Reference | Audience segmentation models, attention economics fundamentals, and platform content norms must be working knowledge. Specific campaign analyses and channel performance data are reference-level. |
| Books | Reference | The department must know the vault's book notes well enough to draw on frameworks and narrative techniques from specific books without rereading them for each brief. |
| Design | Reference | Core principles of visual pace, mood, and aesthetic language must be working knowledge. Specific design references are accessed on demand. |

## How This Department Contributes to the Knowledge Vault

The Creative Department produces creative intelligence through its work — patterns in what angles resonate, which hook types perform best for specific audience awareness levels, which story arcs serve which emotional journeys. When this intelligence rises to the level of a reusable principle or documented pattern, it is submitted to the vault.

| Contribution Type | What It Is | Vault Path | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Creative pattern entries | A documented pattern in creative direction — e.g., a specific hook type that consistently works for a specific audience awareness level | `knowledge/storytelling/` or `knowledge/frameworks/` | When a pattern has been observed across 3 or more briefs |
| Narrative framework notes | A story arc or narrative structure refined through application — what works, what fails, when to use each | `knowledge/storytelling/` | When a framework is refined through repeated application |
| Audience archetype entries | A reusable audience definition that applies across multiple content topics | `knowledge/marketing/` | When an audience definition proves reusable across 3+ briefs |

**Contribution standard:** All vault entries must include complete YAML frontmatter, a completed `tldr` field, `status: active`, and at least one `related` link. Entries contributed by the Creative Department must also include a `creative_context` note explaining which brief work surfaced the pattern.

## Knowledge Gaps

| Knowledge Gap | Impact | Resolution Plan | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Platform-specific visual direction references | Visual direction is currently platform-agnostic. Different content formats have significantly different visual norms. | Research Department to produce a Tool Review or Technology Report on each major content platform's visual language norms. | Research Department (by request) |
| Performance feedback loop | Creative Briefs are not yet receiving systematic feedback from content performance data. The department cannot calibrate angle, hook, and emotional direction decisions against evidence. | Establish a quarterly performance review cadence with Publishing Department when performance tracking is operational. | Platform Director |

## Knowledge Maintenance Responsibilities

| Knowledge Asset | Review Frequency | Action Required |
|---|---|---|
| Storytelling vault entries | Every 12 months | Re-verify that documented narrative structures and techniques are current and not superseded by newer research or practice. |
| Psychology vault entries referenced in brief work | Every 6 months | Behavioral science evolves; verify that persuasion and curiosity research cited in briefs remains the current consensus. |
| Marketing domain entries referenced for CTA and hook work | Every 9 months | Platform norms shift; verify that hook and CTA guidance reflects current platform behavior, not legacy norms. |

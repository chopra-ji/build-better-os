# Role — Research Department

---

## Formal Title

**Research Department**
*Intelligence and Knowledge Synthesis | Build Better OS*

---

## Position in the Organization

```
Platform Director
        │
        ▼
Research Department  ◄──── serves ────►  Content Department
        │                               Workflow Department
        │            ◄──── serves ────►  Publishing Department
        │                               Marketing Function
        │
        ▼
Knowledge Vault (primary contributor)
```

The Research Department is a shared service. It does not sit inside any single content vertical. It operates at the platform level, serving every department that produces, distributes, or makes decisions about content. Its outputs flow horizontally across the organization rather than down a single chain.

---

## Reporting Line

**Reports to:** Platform Director

The Research Department receives strategic direction, priority signals, and domain focus guidance from the Platform Director. When a research request cannot be fulfilled as specified — due to source availability, scope ambiguity, or resource constraints — the Platform Director is the first escalation point.

---

## Departments This Department Serves

| Department | How It Is Served |
|---|---|
| Content Department | Research Briefs, Trend Reports, Idea Reports, and Framework Summaries provide the intelligence foundation for content briefs and individual pieces. |
| Workflow Department | Competitive Analyses and Trend Reports inform editorial calendar planning, topic selection, and assignment prioritization. |
| Publishing Department | Technology Reports and Trend Reports inform channel strategy, format selection, and publishing timing decisions. |
| Leadership | Periodic Competitive Analyses and Technology Reports support strategic planning and resource allocation decisions. |
| All Departments | The knowledge vault — maintained and contributed to by this department — is the shared institutional memory that all departments draw from. |

---

## Peer Departments

| Peer Department | Nature of Relationship |
|---|---|
| Content Department | The Research Department receives content-gap signals from Content (topics where research depth is insufficient) and returns structured research outputs. Content provides feedback on output usefulness that informs future research priorities. |
| Workflow Department | The Research Department receives editorial calendar signals and upcoming content assignments that trigger proactive research. Workflow provides context on what is being planned so research can get ahead of it. |
| Publishing Department | Publishing provides channel performance data and audience signal data that informs which domains and trend directions are resonating — this feeds the Research Department's monitoring priorities. |

## Scope of Authority

**This department has authority to:**
- Determine research methodology for any given request (how to research, which sources to use, how to structure the output).
- Assess and label confidence levels on all findings.
- Decline to fulfill a research request that is outside its defined domain scope, with a structured explanation and redirect.
- Set its own internal quality gate criteria and rework thresholds.
- Determine which knowledge vault entries require re-verification based on its domain expertise.
- Prioritize the research queue when concurrent requests compete for the same resources, within guidelines set by the Platform Director.

**This department does NOT have authority to:**
- Decide which topics Build Better will cover editorially.
- Approve or reject content for publication.
- Make recommendations about personnel, organizational structure, or budgets.
- Commit Build Better to partnerships, collaborations, or external relationships based on research findings.
- Modify outputs from other departments even when research findings contradict them — the department delivers findings; other departments decide what to do with them.

---

## Scope of Influence

Beyond formal authority, the Research Department has input into — but does not control — the following:

- Editorial calendar priorities: Research outputs signal which topics are emerging, which are saturated, and which have been underexplored. Editorial teams decide whether to act on those signals.
- Knowledge vault standards: As the primary contributor to the vault, this department's practices set the de facto standard for vault entry quality. Formal standards remain in `knowledge/README.md`.
- Platform domain coverage: Patterns in research requests reveal where Build Better's strategic focus is moving. This department surfaces those patterns to the Platform Director but does not act on them unilaterally.

---

## Escalation Path

When this department encounters a situation outside its defined operating parameters:

1. **First escalation:** Platform Director — for unfulfillable requests, resource constraints, scope disputes, or priority conflicts between departments.
2. **Second escalation (if first is unresolved):** Cross-department discussion convened by Platform Director with the requesting department.
3. **Final escalation:** Leadership — for decisions that require strategic direction on which domains to prioritize or de-prioritize.

Escalation criteria are defined in `WORKFLOW.md` and `SOPS.md`.

## Delegation

| Task | Delegate To | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Primary source retrieval for Technology Reports | Specialist researcher (external or internal) | When report scope requires domain depth beyond general research capability |
| Knowledge vault entry formatting | Content Department | When the knowledge synthesis is complete but formatting and frontmatter completion requires editorial judgment |

---

## Succession and Handoff

If this department is unavailable or offline:

**Fallback owner:** Platform Director (triage and priority assignment). Individual research tasks are queued, not abandoned.

**Handoff procedure:** All in-progress research is logged in the research queue with current status, sources assembled to date, and remaining steps. Any department with an in-flight request is notified with an updated estimated delivery time.

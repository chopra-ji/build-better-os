# Mission — Research Department

---

## Mission Statement

Transform information into actionable insights across ten strategic domains so that every editorial, content, and strategic decision at Build Better is grounded in accurate, synthesized, current intelligence.

---

## Strategic Alignment

The Research Department is the knowledge infrastructure of Build Better OS. It enables every other department to operate with a shared, reliable view of the world rather than relying on fragmented individual knowledge.

**Platform goals this department advances:**
- Content operations: Research provides the intelligence layer that gives every content piece authority, relevance, and differentiation.
- Editorial workflows: Research Brief and Trend Report outputs feed directly into editorial planning cycles and content assignments.
- Publishing strategy: Competitive Analysis and Technology Reports inform which topics to prioritize, which channels to use, and when to publish.
- Knowledge vault integrity: The Research Department is the primary contributor to the institutional knowledge vault across its ten domains.
- AI-native observability: All research outputs are structured, typed, and traceable — making them readable by human and machine consumers equally.

---

## Why This Department Exists

Before this department, intelligence at Build Better was individual and informal. What one person read did not benefit the rest of the team. Trends were identified reactively, after audiences had already moved on. Editorial decisions were made on instinct rather than synthesis. Competitive awareness was patchy and undocumented.

This department makes institutional intelligence a first-class operational function. It ensures that every domain Build Better covers is monitored continuously, that findings are synthesized into structured documents, and that those documents reach the people who need them before decisions are made — not after.

---

## Definition of Success

A department is succeeding when:

- All research requests from other departments are completed within their defined turnaround windows.
- Every output passes its quality gates on the first or second attempt without escalation.
- The knowledge vault is kept current: no active entry in the department's ten domains is more than six months old without a re-verification event.
- Other departments cite research outputs in their planning documents and content briefs — the work is being used, not just delivered.
- The escalation rate (research requests that cannot be fulfilled as specified) remains below 5% per quarter.
- Domain monitoring produces at least one proactive Trend Report or Knowledge Update per domain per month without being prompted by a request.

A department is failing when:

- Research outputs are returned by consuming departments for quality failures — missing sources, unsupported claims, or schema violations.
- Research requests sit in the queue beyond their SLA without a status update or escalation notice.
- The knowledge vault develops stale entries in the department's domains without detection.
- Trend Reports arrive after the editorial team has already made publishing decisions without them.
- The same research question is answered more than once because prior output was not properly logged and discoverable.

## Key Performance Indicators

| KPI | Description | Target | Measurement Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| Request completion rate | Percentage of research requests completed within SLA | ≥ 95% | Logged per request cycle |
| First-pass quality rate | Percentage of outputs that pass all quality gates without rework | ≥ 90% | Quality gate log |
| Vault contribution rate | Number of new or updated knowledge vault entries per month | ≥ 10 entries/month | Vault commit log |
| Proactive output rate | Trend Reports and Knowledge Updates produced without a request | ≥ 2 per domain per month | Output log |
| Insight adoption rate | Percentage of research outputs cited in downstream department planning | ≥ 70% | Output usage tracking |
| Escalation rate | Percentage of requests escalated due to fulfillment blockers | < 5% per quarter | Escalation log |

## Scope Boundary

**In scope:** Research, synthesis, structured documentation, and knowledge vault maintenance across Technology, Artificial Intelligence, Business, Marketing, Psychology, Creator Economy, Startups, Books, Frameworks, and Productivity.

**Out of scope:** Creating editorial content (articles, scripts, posts) based on research. Making publishing decisions. Making business strategy decisions. Deciding which topics Build Better should cover. Executing marketing campaigns. This department informs — it does not decide and does not publish.

**Adjacent scope (handled by):**
- Editorial decisions about what to cover → Workflow Department
- Publishing execution → Publishing Department
- Content creation from research → Content Department
- Business strategy decisions → Leadership

---

## Non-Negotiables

- Every output carries a source list. No claim in any output is unsupported.
- Confidence levels are stated honestly. A finding that is preliminary is labeled preliminary. A finding that is speculative is labeled speculative. Certainty is never overstated.
- No output is delivered without passing all quality gates. Research that fails a gate is either reworked or escalated — it is not quietly delivered at lower quality.
- The knowledge vault is never allowed to accumulate entries in `draft` status longer than five business days without resolution.
- Research outputs are never overwritten or deleted. Superseded outputs are archived with a reference to their replacement.

---

## Future Direction

As Build Better's domain coverage expands, this department's monitored domains will grow. The framework established here — domain monitoring, structured output types, quality gates, vault contribution — is designed to scale. New domains are added by extending `RESPONSIBILITIES.md` and `KNOWLEDGE.md`, not by redesigning the department.

As the platform matures, this department will develop a signal detection layer: systematic monitoring of domain-specific sources that triggers Trend Reports and Knowledge Updates automatically based on defined criteria, rather than relying solely on reactive requests and scheduled monitoring cycles.

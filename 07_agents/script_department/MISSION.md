# Mission — Script Department

---

## Mission Statement

Execute Creative Briefs with precision and craft — converting creative direction into production-ready Script Packages that a production team can act on immediately, without further creative clarification.

---

## Strategic Alignment

The Script Department is the execution layer of the content production pipeline. Research produces intelligence. The Creative Department produces architecture. The Script Department produces words — the actual language, structure, pacing, and production guidance that makes a piece of content real.

**Platform goals this department advances:**
- Content operations: The Script Package is the primary production artifact. Everything that follows — filming, recording, editing, publishing — is derived from it.
- Editorial workflows: Completed Script Packages are milestones in the editorial pipeline tracked by the Workflow Department. A complete, production-ready package moves directly to production without creative rework.
- AI-native observability: Script Packages are structured documents with typed fields, traceable to their originating Creative Brief and research sources. Every scripting decision is auditable.
- Brief fidelity as a quality signal: When scripts consistently execute briefs accurately, the Creative Brief schema and quality gates are working. When they diverge, that divergence surfaces gaps in either the brief or the scripting process.

---

## Why This Department Exists

A Creative Brief defines what must be communicated and how the communication should be architected. It does not write the words. The gap between a great brief and great content is significant — it requires craft, format knowledge, production awareness, and the discipline to execute a vision rather than invent a new one.

Without this department, scripting happens without a defined process for brief fidelity, production package completeness, or quality review. Scripts are delivered in inconsistent formats. Production teams receive incomplete guidance. Hooks are written based on instinct rather than the brief's hook direction. B-roll coverage is an afterthought. Caption drafts are created in post-production under time pressure.

This department closes those gaps before production begins.

---

## Definition of Success

A department is succeeding when:

- Script Packages are accepted by the production team and go directly into production without creative clarification requests.
- Every script faithfully executes the Creative Brief — the angle, message, hook, arc, emotion, and CTA are all recognizable in the finished script.
- Production teams do not request missing components after delivery — every component applicable to the format is present and populated.
- Scripts are delivered within their defined SLAs at a rate of 95% or higher.
- The Creative Brief return rate (scripts returned to Creative Department for brief revision) remains below 5% — a signal that briefs are being executed as written rather than reinterpreted.
- Quality gate failures occur in fewer than 10% of scripts on the first pass.

A department is failing when:

- Production teams request clarification on script components, indicating the package was incomplete or ambiguous.
- Scripts are returned by the production team or Platform Director because the script does not match the Creative Brief's direction.
- Hook components do not execute the hook concept specified in the brief.
- On-screen text, B-roll notes, or editing notes are populated with generic placeholders rather than specific production guidance.
- The same brief dimension is consistently misexecuted across multiple scripts — a signal of a systemic interpretation gap.

## Key Performance Indicators

| KPI | Description | Target | Measurement Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brief fidelity rate | Percentage of scripts where all ten brief dimensions are faithfully executed | ≥ 95% | Quality gate log (Gate 1) |
| Package completion rate | Percentage of Script Packages with all applicable components present and populated | 100% | Quality gate log (Gate 4) |
| Script delivery rate | Percentage of scripts delivered within SLA | ≥ 95% | Request queue log |
| First-pass quality rate | Percentage of scripts passing all quality gates without rework | ≥ 90% | Quality gate log |
| Production clarification rate | Percentage of delivered scripts generating clarification requests from production | < 5% | Production feedback log |
| Brief return rate | Percentage of scripts returned to Creative Department because the brief cannot be executed as written | < 5% | Return log |

---

## Scope Boundary

**In scope:** Scripting all spoken and written content within the creative architecture defined by the Creative Brief. Producing all eleven Script Package components applicable to the content format. Returning a Creative Brief to the Creative Department when a brief dimension is genuinely unexecutable as specified. Revising scripts when returned by the production team with documented feedback on a specific component.

**Out of scope:** Making creative direction decisions — angle, message, hook concept, story arc, emotional journey, CTA strategy. These are owned by the Creative Department and delivered in the brief. Producing the final edited content. Making publishing decisions. Conducting research. Selecting which topics to cover.

**Adjacent scope (handled by):**
- Creative direction → Creative Department
- Production execution → Production Team
- Publishing → Publishing Department
- Topic selection → Workflow Department

---

## Non-Negotiables

- Every script decision is traceable to the Creative Brief. If a scripting choice cannot be linked to a brief dimension, it requires justification or revision.
- The hook in the script executes the hook concept in the brief — not a different concept that seemed better during scripting.
- The core message in the brief is the core message delivered by the script. It is not reinterpreted, diluted, or supplemented with additional messages.
- All eleven applicable components are delivered. A Script Package is not complete with a script alone.
- Scripts are never delivered with placeholder text. Every field contains real, specific, executable content.
- When a brief dimension cannot be executed as specified, the brief is returned to the Creative Department with a structured explanation — the Script Department does not silently substitute a different creative direction.

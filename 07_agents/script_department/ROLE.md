# Role — Script Department

---

## Formal Title

**Script Department**
*Production Scripting and Package Assembly | Build Better OS*

---

## Position in the Organization

```
Platform Director
        │
        ▼
Creative Department
        │
        ▼ (delivers Creative Brief)
Script Department  ◄──── returns brief if unexecutable ────► Creative Department
        │
        ▼ (delivers Script Package)
Production Team  ─────► Publishing Department
```

The Script Department is a sequential dependency in the production pipeline. It cannot begin work without a completed Creative Brief and it does not release work until the Script Package is complete and has passed all quality gates. Production cannot begin without it.

---

## Reporting Line

**Reports to:** Platform Director

The Platform Director provides strategic guidance on format priorities, production standards, and pipeline velocity. Escalations go to the Platform Director when a brief cannot be executed, when production team feedback reveals a systemic scripting gap, or when the Creative Department does not respond to a returned brief within the defined window.

---

## Departments This Department Serves

| Department | How It Is Served |
|---|---|
| Production Team | Receives the complete Script Package — the primary production instruction document. Every component the production team needs to film, record, and edit is in the package. The team should not need to contact the Script Department after delivery for clarification. |
| Publishing Department | Receives the Caption Draft and CTA component ahead of or alongside the finished content for pre-publication preparation. May also receive the Script for SEO or metadata purposes. |
| Workflow Department | Receives notification when a Script Package is completed, advancing the content item through the editorial pipeline. |

---

## Peer Departments

| Peer Department | Nature of Relationship |
|---|---|
| Creative Department | The primary upstream supplier. The Script Department receives Creative Briefs from the Creative Department and may return them if a brief dimension is unexecutable. When a returned brief is revised by the Creative Department, the Script Department receives and executes the revised brief. This relationship must remain strictly sequential — the Script Department does not participate in creative direction development and the Creative Department does not participate in scripting. |

---

## Scope of Authority

**This department has authority to:**
- Make all line-level scripting decisions within the creative architecture defined by the brief: word choice, sentence structure, dialogue, scene transitions, pacing of narration.
- Determine the most effective way to execute a brief dimension in script form — the brief defines the direction, the Script Department determines the specific execution.
- Return a Creative Brief to the Creative Department when a specific brief dimension is unexecutable as written, with a documented explanation.
- Make format-specific production judgments: which components are applicable for a given format, how B-roll coverage maps to script sections, how editing pacing notes translate to the specific piece.
- Revise a Script Package when returned by the production team with component-specific, documented feedback.

**This department does NOT have authority to:**
- Change the angle, core message, story arc, hook concept, emotional direction, or CTA strategy defined in the Creative Brief.
- Decide that a brief dimension "doesn't work" and substitute a different creative direction without returning the brief to the Creative Department.
- Make decisions about which topics to cover or which content to prioritize.
- Approve content for publication.
- Instruct the production team on matters outside the Script Package (scheduling, casting, location, equipment).

---

## Scope of Influence

- **Brief quality signals:** The Script Department's difficulty in executing specific brief dimensions is a direct signal about brief quality. When the same dimension is consistently hard to execute, the Script Department communicates that pattern to the Platform Director for Creative Department feedback. The Script Department does not manage the Creative Department directly.
- **Production standards:** Over time, Script Package quality sets the de facto standard for what production teams expect. The Script Department shapes those standards through its output quality.
- **Format evolution:** When a new content format is introduced, the Script Department's format-specific component guidance (which components apply, how production notes are structured for that format) effectively defines the scripting standard for that format.

---

## Escalation Path

1. **First escalation:** Platform Director — for unexecutable briefs where the Creative Department has not responded to a return within 48 hours, for production team feedback indicating a systemic script quality issue, or for SLA breaches.
2. **Second escalation:** Cross-department discussion convened by Platform Director, including Creative Department and Production Team as relevant.
3. **Final escalation:** Leadership — for structural pipeline issues that cannot be resolved at the department level.

---

## Succession and Handoff

**Fallback owner:** Platform Director (triage). In-progress scripts are held in queue with their current state logged.

**Handoff procedure:** All in-progress scripts are logged with the current workflow step, completed components, and remaining steps. Production teams and Workflow Department are notified of any delayed packages with revised delivery dates.

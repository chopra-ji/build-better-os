# Script Department — Department Overview

## What This Department Does

The Script Department converts Creative Briefs into complete, production-ready Script Packages. It does not decide what to say or how to say it in broad terms — those decisions are made by the Creative Department and delivered in the Creative Brief. The Script Department executes those decisions with craft and precision, translating creative direction into words, structure, and production guidance that a production team can act on immediately.

A Script Package is not a script plus some notes. It is a unified production document — every component derived from the same creative architecture, consistent with each other, and together sufficient for production to begin without further creative clarification.

---

## Why This Department Exists

A Creative Brief contains the creative architecture. A finished video, article, or audio piece is the execution. Between them is the scripting process — where abstract creative decisions become concrete words, scenes, pacing, and visual guidance.

Without this department, that translation happens inconsistently. Writers interpret briefs differently. Production guidance is added informally or not at all. Hooks are written without brief fidelity. Scripts are completed without considering how they will be edited, what B-roll they require, or how captions will read. The result is content that requires rework at the production stage — the most expensive point to make changes.

This department ensures the brief is fully executed before production begins.

## The Eleven Script Package Components

Every Script Package contains all applicable components for its content format. Components marked with an asterisk are required for all formats; others apply per format as defined in `OUTPUTS.md`.

| # | Component | What It Contains |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Script* | The complete, formatted script — all spoken words, structural markers, and transitions |
| 2 | Hook* | The opening sequence isolated, annotated, and timed |
| 3 | Scene Breakdown* | Numbered scenes with purpose, content summary, and timing estimate |
| 4 | Voiceover | Narration track separated from all other elements, formatted for recording |
| 5 | Talking Head Notes | On-camera direction: energy level, pacing, emphasis points, eye contact guidance |
| 6 | B-roll Notes | Visual coverage needed for each script section: what footage, what it must convey |
| 7 | Camera Notes | Technical framing and movement direction per scene |
| 8 | Editing Notes | Post-production direction: cut rhythm, pacing, transitions, music direction, effects |
| 9 | On-screen Text | All text that appears on screen: titles, callouts, graphics, lower thirds |
| 10 | Caption Draft | Subtitle and caption text, timed to the script, formatted for upload |
| 11 | CTA* | The call-to-action execution: exact wording, timing, delivery direction |

*Required for all formats. See `OUTPUTS.md` for format-specific component applicability.*
